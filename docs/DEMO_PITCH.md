# Demo and Pitch

## 1. Positioning

### One sentence

> Founder Build in Public turns the work and learning a solo founder already does into a newsletter-style Founder Digest and a faceless, review-ready Founder Reel.

### Tagline

> Building in public should be an output of building—not another time-consuming job.

### Technical thesis

> The system reconstructs a founder’s day from curated evidence, enforces workspace privacy boundaries, produces a structured editorial plan, and compiles it into media.

## 2. What the judges need to understand

Within ten seconds:

- builders already produce useful raw material by learning and shipping;
- turning it into content forces them into a second job;
- one command creates two polished, review-ready drafts.

Within sixty seconds:

- newsletter selection is human-guided;
- the agent reasons over structured evidence;
- private work is excluded;
- Remotion renders a real video;
- the submission video itself was produced by the tool.

Open the digest, then play the video.

## 4. Submission-video narrative

The video should be generated by the product from the day’s real but sanitized activities.

### Recommended 55–60 second script

**0–6 seconds — Hook**

Today started with football, newsletters and coffee—and ended with the video you are watching.

**6–15 seconds — Problem**

Solo founders are builders, not full-time content creators. Turning what they learn and ship into public content becomes another time-consuming job.

**15–25 seconds — Existing habit**

I already read every useful newsletter in Gmail. I usually curate the best ideas, write down what I learned, and decide what to explore next.

**25–38 seconds — Product**

So I built Founder Build in Public. I select the useful emails, and the agent extracts the key ideas, connects them to my projects, and stores the learning in a structured founder log.

**38–49 seconds — Agentic output**

It combines that learning with what I built today, removes private workspace details, and creates two drafts: a newsletter-style Founder Digest and a faceless Founder Reel.

**49–57 seconds — Reveal**

This script, narration, scene plan, and video were generated by that same workflow.

**57–60 seconds — Closing**

Building in public should be an output of building—not another time-consuming job.

## 5. Scene plan

| Time | Scene | Visual |
|---|---|---|
| 0–6s | Hook | Animated cards: football, newsletter, coffee, terminal |
| 6–15s | Problem | Strong kinetic typography |
| 15–25s | Existing habit | Gmail-like sanitized newsletter cards |
| 25–38s | Product | Terminal command and pipeline animation |
| 38–49s | Outputs | Browser mockup of digest + vertical video preview |
| 49–57s | Self-reference | “This video was generated by the agent” |
| 57–60s | Tagline | Product name and final line |

Do not use copyrighted football broadcast footage. Use a simple football icon, scoreboard-style card, or text-based moment.

## 6. Sixty-second screening video structure

If the formal submission video itself must explain the project, use:

- 0–8s: founder problem;
- 8–18s: one command;
- 18–34s: curation and reasoning stages;
- 34–48s: two outputs;
- 48–56s: self-generated reveal;
- 56–60s: tagline and repository name.

## 10. Closing pitch

> Builders already produce the raw material for good content every day, but a one-person company cannot afford a content team. Founder Build in Public turns founder-approved learning and work into a coherent, public-safe story, then delivers a newsletter-style Founder Digest and a faceless Founder Reel—without asking the founder to become a full-time content creator.
